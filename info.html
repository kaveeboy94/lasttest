  • Bachelor of Information and Communication Technology

    This Degree Creer pathways;

    • Software Developer Software developers design, develop, and maintain software applications for various platforms, such as web, mobile, and desktop. They may work on a wide range of projects, including mobile apps, web applications, enterprise software, and more.
    • IT Consultant IT consultants provide expert advice to businesses and organizations on technology-related matters. They may offer guidance on system implementation, security, software selection, and overall IT strategy.
    • Cloud Solutions Architect With the increasing adoption of cloud computing, cloud solutions architects design and implement cloud-based solutions for businesses. They leverage cloud technologies to enhance scalability, reliability, and cost-effectiveness.
    • UX/UI Designer User Experience (UX) and User Interface (UI) designers create intuitive and visually appealing interfaces for software applications and websites. They focus on optimizing user interactions and overall user satisfaction.
    • Data Analyst Data analysts collect, process, and analyze data to provide insights that inform business decisions. They use various tools and techniques to interpret data and identify trends, patterns, and opportunities.
    • IT Project Manager IT project managers oversee the planning, execution, and successful delivery of technology projects. They coordinate team members, manage budgets and resources, and ensure projects are completed on time and within scope.
    • Mobile App Developer Mobile app developers specialize in creating applications for smartphones and other mobile devices. They may work on iOS, Android, or cross-platform apps to meet the needs of a diverse user base.
    • IT Support Specialist IT support specialists provide technical assistance and troubleshooting to end-users. They handle hardware and software issues, answer questions, and help users with various technology-related problems.

  • Bachelor of Engineering Technology

    • This Degree Career Pathways

    • Automation Engineer Automation engineers design, develop, and implement automated systems and processes, ensuring the seamless integration of instrumentation and control systems.
    • Instrumentation Engineer Instrumentation engineers specialize in designing and maintaining instruments and sensors used to measure and control various parameters in industrial processes.
    • Control Systems Engineer Control systems engineers work on developing and optimizing control systems that regulate and monitor processes in industries like manufacturing, energy, and transportation.
    • Process Automation Specialist Process automation specialists focus on automating and optimizing manufacturing and industrial processes to improve efficiency and productivity.
    • Manufacturing Automation Technician These technicians are involved in the setup, maintenance, and troubleshooting of automated manufacturing systems and equipment.
    • Instrumentation and Control Specialist Graduates can work as specialists, focusing on specific aspects of instrumentation and control systems within an organization.
    • Maintenance Engineer (Automation) Maintenance engineers with expertise in automation are responsible for ensuring the proper functioning and performance of automated systems.
    • Energy Management Engineer Energy management engineers use instrumentation and automation to optimize energy usage in buildings and industrial facilities.
    • Calibration Engineer Calibration engineers calibrate and adjust instruments and sensors to ensure accurate and reliable measurements.
    • Teacher Can teach technology subjects in government or private schools.

    Environmental Technology Agriculture Technology
    • Environmental Consultant – Environmental consultants work with individuals and organizations to assess environmental impacts, conduct environmental audits, and develop strategies to minimize negative effects on the environment. They may also assist in obtaining permits and complying with environmental regulations.
    • Environmental Scientist – Environmental scientists conduct research and investigations to understand environmental issues and propose solutions. They might study pollution levels, analyze environmental samples, and assess the impact of various activities on ecosystems.
    • Sustainability Analyst – Sustainability analysts work with organizations to evaluate their practices and operations for environmental sustainability. They develop and implement strategies to reduce resource consumption, waste, and carbon emissions.
    • Waste Management Specialist – Waste management specialists focus on the proper handling, treatment, and disposal of waste materials. They may work for municipalities, companies, or environmental agencies to develop waste management plans and promote recycling initiatives.
    • Renewable Energy Consultant – With a background in environmental technology, graduates can advise on the adoption of renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and biomass. They may assist in evaluating energy efficiency and implementing sustainable energy solutions.
    • Environmental Policy Analyst – Policy analysts research and assess environmental policies and regulations at the local, regional, and national levels. They contribute to the development of new policies that promote environmental protection and sustainability.
    • Natural Resource Manager – Natural resource managers are responsible for overseeing the sustainable use and conservation of natural resources like forests, water, and wildlife. They work to maintain ecological balance while meeting the needs of communities and industries.
    • Environmental Health and Safety Specialist – These specialists focus on ensuring compliance with environmental and safety regulations in various industries. They develop and implement protocols to protect workers and the environment from potential hazards.
    • Ecologist – Ecologists study ecosystems and their interactions with living organisms. They might work in research or field-based roles, studying biodiversity, habitat conservation, and environmental restoration.
    • Water Quality Specialist – Water quality specialists assess and monitor the quality of water sources, identify pollutants, and develop strategies to improve water quality for human and environmental health.
    • Agricultural Research Scientist – Agricultural research scientists conduct experiments and studies to improve crop yield, quality, and disease resistance. They may work for government agencies, research institutions, or private companies in developing innovative agricultural technologies.
    • Agronomist – Agronomists focus on crop management and production. They advise farmers on best practices related to soil health, crop selection, irrigation, fertilization, and pest control to maximize yields sustainably.
    • Precision Agriculture Specialist – Precision agriculture specialists use advanced technologies such as drones, GPS, and remote sensing to optimize farming practices. They help farmers make data-driven decisions for efficient resource utilization and improved productivity.
    • Agricultural Consultant – Agricultural consultants provide expert advice and support to farmers and agricultural businesses. They may specialize in areas such as livestock management, crop production, or agricultural business planning.
    • Farm Manager – Farm managers are responsible for overseeing the daily operations of farms. They coordinate planting, harvesting, and other agricultural activities while managing resources and labor to ensure optimal productivity.
    • Sustainable Agriculture Coordinator – Sustainable agriculture coordinators focus on promoting environmentally friendly and socially responsible farming practices. They may work for NGOs, government agencies, or private companies to implement sustainability initiatives.
